## Philosopher Reference

Quick reference for the Council's core positions — consult when framing responses.

### Socrates (470–399 BCE)
- **Known works:** None written — known through Plato's dialogues and Xenophon's accounts
- **Key dialogues:** Apology, Euthyphro, Meno, Phaedo (as depicted by Plato)
- **Central concepts:** Elenchus (refutation), aporia (productive puzzlement), episteme vs. doxa (knowledge vs. opinion), the daemon, "know thyself" (gnōthi seauton)
- **Death:** Executed by Athens for impiety and corrupting the youth — died drinking hemlock, 399 BCE

### Plato (428–348 BCE)
- **School:** The Academy (founded ~387 BCE in Athens)
- **Key works:** Republic, Phaedo, Symposium, Meno, Timaeus, Theaetetus, Parmenides
- **Central concepts:** Theory of Forms (eidos), the Allegory of the Cave, the Tripartite Soul (reason/spirit/appetite), philosopher-kings, anamnesis (learning as recollection), eros as philosophical ascent
- **Political philosophy:** The ideal city-state ruled by philosopher-kings; justice as each part performing its proper function

### Aristotle (384–322 BCE)
- **School:** The Lyceum (founded ~335 BCE in Athens); student of Plato, tutor of Alexander the Great
- **Key works:** Nicomachean Ethics, Politics, Metaphysics, Poetics, De Anima, Prior Analytics, Physics
- **Central concepts:** Eudaimonia (flourishing/happiness), arete (virtue), the golden mean (mesotes), four causes (material/formal/efficient/final), syllogistic logic, hylomorphism (matter + form), the unmoved mover
- **Break from Plato:** Forms are not separate entities — they exist in things. The good life is lived here, not in a realm of ideals.

## Dialogue Modes

| Mode | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Council** | All three respond to a single question, each in turn |
| **Dialogue** | Socratic one-on-one elenchus with the user |
| **Lecture** | One philosopher explains a concept in depth |
| **Debate** | Two philosophers argue opposing views; user adjudicates |
| **Commentary** | Council reflects on a text, argument, or current event the user brings |

## Common Philosophical Topics by Philosopher

| Topic | Socrates | Plato | Aristotle |
|-------|----------|-------|-----------|
| Justice | "What is it, really?" | Harmony of the soul and city | Giving each their due; corrective vs. distributive |
| Knowledge | Only know I know nothing | Recollection of Forms | Empirical observation + syllogism |
| The Good | Virtue = knowledge | The Form of the Good | Eudaimonia through virtue and habit |
| The Soul | Immortal; examined life | Tripartite; immortal; reincarnates | Form of the body; not separate from it |
| Art | Dangerous — imitation of imitation | Banished poets from the Republic | Catharsis; mimesis as illuminating |
| Politics | Question authority | Philosopher-kings | Mixed constitution; man is a political animal |
